Money transfer abroad

Note!!! Since your salary will be paid to a Uzcard Sum Debit Card from the NBU, be aware that you cannot directly transfer money to an international bank account or any other international card.

Via a Visa Card from NBU

You can open a Visa card with NBU alongside your Uzcard Sum Debit Card. Through the NBU mobile application, you can exchange Uzbek Sum to US Dollars. From your Visa card, you can then transfer money to another Visa card abroad.

Pros Time efficient and quick Easy to track and manage
Cons You cannot transfer funds directly to a bank account if you do not have a Visa card. Small transaction fee, usually 1%

Via SWIFT Transfer

Note!!! Your salary card is not the same as a bank account. A separate bank account is required for international transfers


You can also make SWIFT transactions from the same NBU branch where your Uzcard sum debit card is issued.


To do this, you must first open a bank account at the branch. Once the account is set up, the bank will transfer funds from your salary card to your bank account, from which the SWIFT transaction can be processed.

Required documents for the transfer include:

  • Passport
  • Your Uzcard Sum Debit Card
  • Income verification ( more about Income verification is here)

Note: These documents are required for every SWIFT transaction. During your first application, the bank will create a SWIFT account linked to your NBU app. They will top up your bank account from your Humo Sum Debit Card and initiate the transfer. The service fee is 0.5% of the transfer amount, plus an additional 90,000 Sum (approximately 8 USD).
