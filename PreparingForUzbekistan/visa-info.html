Visa Information for Uzbekistan

Your guide to obtaining the correct visa for work and family visits.

Types of Visas

When traveling to Uzbekistan for work or visiting family, it's essential to apply for the **correct visa**. Below are the most common visa types:

  • A-2 Working Educational Visa: Issued for foreign faculty members employed in Uzbekistan. This visa is required for those officially hired by an educational institution.
  • PV-2 Visiting Visa: Issued for immediate family members of faculty members. Spouses and children of faculty members need this visa for legal stay in Uzbekistan.

Visa Details

The table below outlines the key details of both visa types, including who can apply, validity, and extension requirements.

Visa Type Who Can Apply? Duration Extension Process
A-2 Working Visa Foreign faculty members Up to 365 days To extend the visa, employees **do not need to leave Uzbekistan**. However, they must apply for an extension **at least one month before** the visa expires. Required documents: passport, work contract, and employment verification letter.
PV-2 Visiting Visa Spouses and children of faculty members Up to 365 days Similar to the A-2 visa, family members **do not need to leave Uzbekistan** for an extension. Applications must be submitted **one month before expiration** with proof of relationship (marriage/birth certificates).

How to Apply for a Visa?

Follow these steps to successfully obtain your visa:

  1. Check Eligibility: Ensure you are applying for the correct visa type based on your status (faculty or family member).
  2. Prepare Required Documents: Passport, employment contract (for A-2), or proof of relationship (for PV-2).
  3. Submit Application: Apply at the Uzbekistan embassy or consulate in your home country.
  4. Receive Visa: Processing times vary, so apply well in advance (typically takes 2-4 weeks).
  5. Travel to Uzbekistan: Upon arrival, ensure your visa details are correct and valid.
